Hello, I'm 29 y/o game-programmer student at Forsberg's School.

I have been into sales and service but past 10 years but after deep and long consideration I decided to change career path and game programming was a good place to start since gaming is my hobby and I thought what's better than learning more about something you enjoy and make a living of it.

I'm not entirely sure yet but I think my favorite things are Transformations and Shaders probably because Math is not my strongest suit and these areas offers most challenge and growth opportunity for me.

Since joining Forsberg I've learned to work in object oriented manner, creating custom classes, functions and optimization patterns, I also learned little about different algorithms and their use cases. Probably most important thing I gained was general understanding of how teams work together to create something great, and that perfection is in the details.
