Abdulakh Kurbanov

Hello, I'm 29 y/o game-programmer student at Forsberg's School.

I have been into sales and service past 10 years but I decided to change career path and game programming was a good place to start since gaming, sci-fi and fantasy stories are my hobby so I thought what's better than learning more about something you enjoy and make a living of it.

Recently I've discovered that my favorite thing is gameplay mechanics, to make abilities and combat systems, and the thing I'm least excited about is SFX but I do think VFX is cool.

Since joining Forsberg I've learned to work in object oriented manner, creating custom classes, functions and optimization patterns, I also learned about different algorithms and their use cases.

Probably most important thing I gained was general understanding of how teams work together to create something great, and polishing a game is all about digging deep into the details.

The funny thing is, I used to question what's possible when it comes to coding and creating but now I think anything is possible I just have to learn how to do it.

As a complete beginner I started with Unity & C# but even tho Unity has it's charm I like Unreal Engine more.

My aim for near future is to get better with UE5, C++ and learn Blender.
